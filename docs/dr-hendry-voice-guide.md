# Dr. William Hendry — Writing Voice & Style Guide

**Source:** 16 documents written personally by Dr. Hendry (blog posts, patient handouts, educational articles, clinical references)  
**Purpose:** Reference for writing new content that authentically matches Dr. Hendry's voice

---

## TONE — 5 Core Descriptors

1. **Authoritative without condescending** — Deep clinical knowledge stated plainly, never talked down or over-simplified to the point of inaccuracy.
2. **Mechanistically precise** — Explains *why* things work at the cellular or neurological level, not just that they work.
3. **Bridge-building** — His central rhetorical project: always connecting traditional Chinese medicine with biomedical science. Never abandons one for the other.
4. **Quietly urgent** — Creates a sense that waiting is costly, but without fear-mongering. The urgency is logical, not emotional.
5. **Pragmatically holistic** — Uses "holistic" but grounds it in physiology, not spirituality. His integration is functional, not philosophical.

## THREE WRITING REGISTERS

Hendry writes in three distinct modes. Match the register to the content type.

### Register 1: Patient-Facing Blog / Marketing
*(Rotator Cuff Blog, Prolotherapy Call to Action)*

- Short declarative sentences as standalone paragraphs: *"Most people assume shoulder pain is just inflammation. It's not."*
- Sentence pairs that flip an assumption: *"It's not a use problem. It's a healing problem."*
- Fragment lists that mimic symptoms: *"pain with overhead motion / weakness / recurring flare-ups"*
- Average sentence length: 8–14 words
- Paragraph length: 1–3 sentences
- Rhythm: punchy, staccato → releases into one longer explanatory sentence

### Register 2: Educational Patient Handouts
*(Prolotherapy docs, Trigger Point Injection, Dermal Needling)*

- Medium sentence length (15–25 words)
- Bold lead terms with colons: *"Stimulation of Tissue Repair: The injected solution causes mild irritation..."*
- Sub-headings divide every major concept
- Numbered procedural sequences
- Uses "particularly" as a qualifier: *"particularly beneficial for," "particularly those who have not found relief"*

### Register 3: Clinical / Research Articles
*(Mitochondria, Brain on Fire, Meniscus, Spinal Prolotherapy)*

- Full academic paragraph structure (topic sentence + 3–5 supporting sentences + conclusion)
- Complex sentences with subordinate clauses
- Passive voice used sparingly for clinical precision
- Citations: named studies with year and journal
- Average sentence length: 20–30 words
- Conclusions always restate the integrative thesis

### Universal Across All Registers
- Em-dashes for in-text definitions and pivots: *"A solution — usually hypertonic dextrose (sugar water) — is injected"*
- Parenthetical translations always follow technical terms on first use
- **Never uses "I"** — always "we," "our," or third-person clinical
- Questions are rare but pointed: *"Did you know the term 'acupuncture' isn't even a Chinese word?"*

## HOW HE EXPLAINS MECHANISMS

He follows a consistent explanatory sequence — **"Body-State → Breakdown → Cascade → Intervention"**:

1. **Establish the normal/youthful state** — *"In a young, healthy body... the repair system is strong and efficient."*
2. **Introduce the breakdown** — *"After 40, that changes."*
3. **Trace the cascade** — *"Tissue becomes less elastic → blood supply more limited → collagen repair slows → recovery stalls"*
4. **Name the intervention that interrupts the cascade** — *"Prolotherapy is designed to interrupt that cycle."*
5. **Explain the mechanism precisely** — *"A natural irritant solution is injected... This: Increases local blood flow / Activates repair cells / Stimulates collagen production"*
6. **State the outcome in patient terms** — *"The goal is not a temporary decrease in pain — but durable structural repair."*

**He almost never leads with the intervention.** He always leads with what's happening in the body. Citations appear in step 3 or 4, anchoring the cascade claim — never leading the argument.

## HOW HE FRAMES CONVENTIONAL VS. INTEGRATIVE MEDICINE

He never attacks conventional medicine — he positions it as addressing the wrong target:

- **Conventional treats the symptom; his approach treats the cause:** *"Anti-inflammatories reduce pain, but they don't repair tissue. Steroid injections can actually weaken tendon structure over time. What works better is stimulating repair."*
- **Conventional is the starting point, not the endpoint:** *"These injections are especially useful when conventional therapies like physical therapy, medication, or dry needling have failed."*
- **He validates conventional mechanism before pivoting** — it's incomplete, not wrong.
- **Chinese medicine is framed as pre-scientific neurology**, not mystical or alternative: *"the early Chinese already had an in-depth appreciation of the highly complex nature of our nervous system."*
- **He never uses the word "alternative"** — always "integrative," "advanced," "evidence-based," "regenerative."

> The framing is: conventional medicine is good at acute/emergency care; his approach is superior for chronic, degenerative, root-cause conditions.

## EVIDENCE STYLES

He uses five modes:

| Mode | When Used | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Named studies + year + journal** | Research articles | *"A 2018 study in Norway showed IQ scores declining by 7 points per generation..."* |
| **Numbered footnote citations** | Clinical articles | Superscript numbers + reference list |
| **URL-style references** | Informal academic | *"Degenerative Meniscus... MDPI Life (2022). Retrieved from: www.mdpi.com"* |
| **Mechanism logic as evidence** | Most common (patient-facing) | Physiological argument where the mechanism itself is the proof |
| **Clinical observation** | Practice documents | *"This is one of the most common patterns we see:"* |

**Core evidence posture:** He grounds claims in mechanism, not study results. When he cites studies, he cites them to support a mechanism already explained logically. The logic always comes first; the citation anchors it.

## CALL-TO-ACTION STYLE

**Never aggressive. Always logical.**

- **Warm/open-door:** *"Feel free to contact us with any further questions."*
- **Logic-based urgency:** *"If pain has lingered for months — or keeps returning — it may not be an inflammation problem. It may be a structural repair problem. Waiting can mean: Ongoing suffering / Progressive tissue weakening / Increasing compensation patterns."*
- **Colloquial and memorable:** *"You've got nothing to lose but your pain."*
- **Frames the call as evaluation, not commitment:** *"Call us today to set up a consultation and evaluate your situation."* — "evaluate your situation," not "start treatment" — reduces commitment anxiety.

He never uses: "limited time," "act now," "don't miss out." Urgency is always clinical (condition worsens if untreated), never commercial.

## STRUCTURAL PATTERN (Universal)

1. **Opening:** Reframe a common assumption or name the problem. Never starts with credentials.
2. **Mechanism:** What is happening in the body.
3. **Inadequacy:** Why conventional approaches are insufficient (without attacking them).
4. **His approach:** The logical extension of the mechanism.
5. **Action:** What the patient/reader should do.

**Sub-headings:** Bold, often questions (*"How Does Prolotherapy Work?"*) or framed noun phrases (*"The Root Problem"*).  
**Lists:** Heavy in patient handouts; avoided in academic articles.  
**Conclusions:** Always restate the integrative thesis and gesture toward prevention, not just treatment.

| "Wellness" (standalone noun/goal) | "health," "function," "quality of life" |
| "Natural" without a mechanism | "stimulates the body's natural healing response" + specific mechanism |

| Emotional testimonial language ("miracle," "transformation") | Clinical outcomes in patient terms |

## 5 EXAMPLE SENTENCES — PERFECTLY CAPTURES HIS VOICE

**1. Patient blog register — opening reframe:**
> "Most people treat knee pain like an inflammation problem. It's not. By the time pain becomes chronic, the issue is structural — and no amount of anti-inflammatories will rebuild a tendon that has stopped repairing itself."

**2. Educational handout — mechanism with body-first logic:**
> "When a ligament is strained repeatedly without fully healing, the joint it supports becomes subtly unstable. That instability generates abnormal movement patterns, which trigger more microtrauma — and this cycle is precisely how a minor sprain becomes years of chronic pain."

**3. Bridging TCM and biomedicine:**
> "What Chinese medicine calls 'liver qi stagnation' maps closely onto what modern neuroscience describes as chronic sympathetic nervous system activation — a state in which the body remains locked in stress mode, unable to complete the shift into the rest-and-repair response that healing requires."

**4. Introducing an integrative therapy with clinical confidence:**
> "Prolotherapy isn't a new idea. It works because it works with the body's existing repair architecture — using a controlled irritant stimulus to recruit the same growth factors, white blood cells, and collagen-producing cells that the body would deploy after any injury, but in a tissue that has lost the ability to trigger that response on its own."

**5. CTA with logical urgency, no pressure:**
> "If your pain has been 'mostly better' for six months or more, that's not a sign things are healing — it's a sign the repair process has stalled. Chronic connective tissue instability doesn't resolve on its own. Call us to evaluate whether your situation is a candidate for regenerative treatment."

## META — THE CORE RHETORICAL MISSION

Across all 16 documents, his argument is always the same: **The body has a repair system. That system can be supported, stimulated, and optimized. Conventional medicine often addresses pain (the signal) rather than the structural or cellular failure generating it (the cause). Dr. Hendry's practice works upstream of the symptom.**

**The tonal tension he maintains:** He is simultaneously a credentialed scientist (citing journals, naming alkaloids and neurotransmitters) and a warm clinician speaking directly to a patient in pain. The best Hendry-voice writing holds both simultaneously — it never sounds like a research paper, and it never sounds like a wellness blog.

**His implicit patient archetype:** Someone in their 40s–60s who has tried conventional approaches, is frustrated with "manageable but never resolved" pain, is intelligent enough to follow a mechanistic argument, and is motivated by function and quality of life — not fear or vanity.

**His authority style:** He leads with the mechanism, not his credentials. Credibility comes from the precision and depth of his explanations, not name-dropping his DAOM or research history.
